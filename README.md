# Geospatial Analysis of Bar Success in Philadelphia Using Big Data

## Project Overview

Location plays a critical role in the success of restaurants and bars. Even established brands can fail if they overlook strategic site selection. This project investigates where a new bar in Philadelphia should be located to maximize customer satisfaction and business potential.

Using Yelp’s open dataset and U.S. Census data, we analyzed thousands of bars and over one million customer reviews to identify patterns in:

* Customer satisfaction
* Popular bar features
* Neighborhood demographics
* Geographic “opportunity zones”

The goal was to develop data-driven insights and visualization tools that help prospective bar owners make better location decisions.

## Business Understanding

Opening a bar involves high financial risk, especially in competitive cities like Philadelphia. Poor site selection can lead to low foot traffic, negative reviews, and business failure.

This project aims to answer a key business question:
**Where should a new bar be opened in Philadelphia to maximize success?**

By analyzing review sentiment, bar attributes, and neighborhood characteristics, the project identifies areas with strong customer satisfaction but low competition, providing valuable insights for entrepreneurs and investors.

## Techniques Used

### Exploratory Data Analysis
* Bar charts
* Histograms
* Scatter plots

### Topic Modeling
Applied BERTopic on over 200,000 reviews to identify common themes influencing customer satisfaction.

### Geospatial Analysis
Created interactive Tableau maps to visualize:
* Bar density
* Average ratings by census tract
* Opportunity zones for new bars

### Feature Analysis
Examined correlations between bar features (Wi-Fi, outdoor seating, happy hour, etc.) and ratings.

## Key Insights
### 1. Strong Concepts Drive Success
Bars with clear culinary identity and strong service (e.g., sushi, vegan options) tend to receive higher ratings.

### 2. Gimmicks Don’t Guarantee Satisfaction
Themes focused heavily on novelty (e.g., rooftop bars or tech-heavy ordering) often received lower ratings.

### 3. High Popularity ≠ High Quality
Frequently mentioned topics did not always correspond to high ratings, showing that visibility alone does not guarantee customer satisfaction. 

### 4. Opportunity Zones Exist
Certain census tracts showed:
* High average ratings
* Low bar density

These areas represent potentially profitable locations for new bar openings.
